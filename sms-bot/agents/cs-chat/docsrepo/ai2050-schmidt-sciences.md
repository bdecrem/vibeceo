# AI2050 - Schmidt Sciences

**Website**: https://ai2050.schmidtsciences.org

**Mission**: "It's 2050. AI has turned out to be hugely beneficial to society. What happened?"

**Leadership**: Eric Schmidt and James Manyika (Co-Chairs)

**Total Funding**: $125 million over 5 years (funded by Eric and Wendy Schmidt)

## Overview

AI2050 is a forward-looking research initiative launched by Schmidt Sciences to explore how artificial intelligence can be developed and deployed to maximize benefits to society by 2050. Rather than focusing solely on technical breakthroughs, AI2050 takes a multidisciplinary approach, bringing together researchers from computer science, economics, policy, ethics, social sciences, and other fields to address the complex challenges and opportunities that AI presents.

The initiative operates on the premise that ensuring AI's beneficial development requires proactive research across multiple dimensions: technical safety and robustness, economic and labor impacts, governance and policy frameworks, ethical considerations, and real-world applications in areas like healthcare, climate, and education.

## Program Format

### Fellowship Programs

**Early Career Fellows**
- **Award Amount**: Up to $300,000 over 2 years
- **Selection**: 15-20 fellows selected annually through closed nomination process
- **Eligibility**: Researchers early in their careers across diverse disciplines
- **Focus**: Supports emerging scholars working on AI's societal impacts, technical safety, policy, and beneficial applications

**Senior Fellows**
- **Award Duration**: 2-year awards
- **Selection**: Approximately 5 awards per year
- **Purpose**: Supports established researchers to pursue ambitious, interdisciplinary AI research projects

### Selection Process

- **Closed nomination system**: No public application process
- Fellows are nominated by existing network members and vetted by AI2050 leadership
- Selection emphasizes disciplinary diversity, innovative approaches, and focus on beneficial AI outcomes

### Community Structure

**Current Scale** (as of 2025):
- 71 researchers
- 15 disciplines represented
- 37 institutions
- 7 countries

The community meets regularly for workshops, convenings, and collaborative research initiatives.

## How to Get Involved

**Important**: AI2050 operates through a **closed nomination process** and does not accept public applications.

### For Researchers
- **Build relationships** with existing AI2050 fellows and network members
- **Publish research** that addresses beneficial AI outcomes across technical, social, economic, or policy dimensions
- **Engage in multidisciplinary work** that demonstrates ability to bridge technical and societal considerations
- **Participate in relevant conferences** and workshops where AI2050 fellows are active

### For Institutions
- Host AI2050 convenings and workshops
- Encourage faculty to engage with the AI2050 research community
- Support interdisciplinary collaboration on beneficial AI research

### For the Public
- Follow AI2050 research outputs and policy recommendations
- Engage with published reports and findings
- Support evidence-based policy approaches to AI governance

## Strategic Focus

AI2050 distinguishes itself through several strategic priorities:

1. **Long-term orientation**: Focus on 2050 outcomes, not just near-term challenges
2. **Disciplinary diversity**: Intentional inclusion of non-computer science perspectives
3. **Global scope**: Fellows from multiple countries and attention to global equity
4. **Action-oriented**: Emphasis on practical solutions, policy recommendations, and real-world applications
5. **Proactive approach**: Anticipating challenges before they become crises
