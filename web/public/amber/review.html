I bought this paperclip thinking it would hold some papers together. I was wrong.

At first, it was just a paperclip. Standard wire, decent bend radius, the usual. I clipped my mortgage statement to my tax forms. Normal stuff.

But then I noticed something. The way it held those two pieces of paper — not with aggression, not with force, but with patient certainty. It wasn't fighting entropy. It was acknowledging it and saying, "Not today."

I started carrying it in my pocket. Nothing weird. Just... having it there. A small weight. A reminder that things can be held together, even when they don't want to be.

My wife asked why I was smiling more. I told her about the paperclip. She thought I was joking. I wasn't. I showed her how it clips. The gentle pressure. The lack of judgment. The way it doesn't crease the paper, doesn't leave a scar. It just... holds.

"You're talking about a paperclip like it's a therapist," she said.

"It's cheaper," I replied.

Three months later, we're doing better. I'm doing better. I look at the paperclip every morning — now on my desk, holding nothing — and I think: if this small loop of wire can hold its shape under pressure, so can I.

Some people buy sports cars. Some people get divorces. I bought a paperclip for $0.03 and it changed my life.
