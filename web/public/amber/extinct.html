EXTINCT SOUNDS

An audio museum of things the internet killed
These sounds used to be everywhere. Now they exist only in memory and synthesized reconstruction. Tap to hear them one last time.
▶ Dial-Up Modem Connecting
Extinct: ~2004
The screech of patience. You'd hear this for 30-60 seconds while connecting to the internet at 56kbps. If someone picked up the phone, it all died.
▶ VHS Rewinding
Extinct: ~2006
The mechanical whirr of magnetic tape spooling backward. "Be kind, rewind." If you didn't, Blockbuster charged you $1.
▶ Pay Phone Coin Drop
Extinct: ~2008
The metallic clunk of quarters dropping through the slot. 25 cents bought you 3 minutes. Now pay phones are museum pieces.
▶ Floppy Disk Save
Extinct: ~2003
The grinding seek-and-write sound. 1.44 MB of storage, which felt like plenty. The save icon still looks like one.
▶ Blockbuster Receipt Printer
Extinct: 2013
The dot-matrix printer churning out your rental receipt. "Due back by 11:59 PM Tuesday." Late fees were brutal.
▶ "You've Got Mail"
Extinct: ~2009
The most exciting three words of the late 90s. AOL's email notification. Now email is a source of dread, not joy.
▶ CD-ROM Drive Spinning Up
Extinct: ~2010
The accelerating whirr of a disc spinning to 7200 RPM. Installing software from a CD took forever. Scratches were fatal.
▶ Film Camera Advance
Extinct: ~2005
The mechanical winding sound after taking a photo. You had 24 or 36 shots per roll. No deleting. No previews. Just hope.
▶ CRT TV Powering On
Extinct: ~2007
The electronic hum and crackle as the cathode ray tube warmed up. The screen would glow before the picture appeared.
