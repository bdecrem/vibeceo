Fundraising

Listen up. I'm going to tell you what no one else will about raising money. No sugar-coating.

Truth #1: Fundraising is not validation. It's just money. The market validates you, not VCs.

Before You Even Think About Raising

Do you actually need money? Most of you don't. You need customers. Bootstrap until it hurts, then bootstrap some more. Every dollar you raise is a piece of your company you'll never get back.

The Brutal Reality Check

Here's what fundraising actually looks like:

  • You'll pitch 100+ investors
  • 95 will ghost you
  • 4 will string you along for months
  • 1 might give you money

And that's if you're lucky.

Truth #2: VCs are not your friends. They're not your mentors. They're financial instruments looking for 10x returns. Act accordingly.

What Actually Matters

Traction trumps everything. A shitty deck with real revenue beats a beautiful deck with a "vision" every single time.

Stop perfecting your pitch deck. Start perfecting your product. Here's your priority list:

  1. Monthly recurring revenue
  2. Customer growth rate
  3. Retention metrics
  4. Everything else

The Only Pitch That Works

Forget the 30-slide deck. Here's your pitch:

"We make [product] for [customer]. We charge [price]. We're growing [X]% month-over-month. We need [amount] to do [specific thing] which will get us to [concrete milestone]."

That's it. If they want more, they'll ask.

Truth #3: The best time to raise money is when you don't need it. The worst time is when you do.

Negotiation Reality

You have zero leverage unless:

  • Multiple investors want in (create FOMO)
  • You can walk away (have runway)
  • Your numbers are undeniable

The Biggest Mistakes

1. Raising too early: You dilute yourself into oblivion
2. Raising too much: Expectations become crushing
3. Raising from the wrong people: Bad investors are worse than no investors

When to Say No

Walk away if:

  • They want a board seat for less than $1M
  • They push for weird terms (2x liquidation preferences, etc.)
  • Your gut says no (trust it)
  • They're assholes in diligence (it only gets worse)
Truth #4: Every VC will tell you they're "founder-friendly." None of them are when shit hits the fan.

The Only Advice That Matters

Build a business so good that investors beg to give you money. Everything else is just tactics.

Stop reading fundraising blogs. Stop going to pitch events. Stop "networking."

Start building. Start selling. Start growing.

The money will follow.

Remember: The goal isn't to raise money. The goal is to build something people want. Fundraising is just a sometimes-necessary evil on that path.

Now get back to work.
