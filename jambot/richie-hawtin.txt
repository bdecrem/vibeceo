To give your AI producer the best chance of success, you should use **Richie Hawtin (Plastikman)** as your primary reference.

While artists like Boris Brejcha or Robert Hood are legends, Hawtin is the most "documented" artist in the genre. Because he has spent decades acting as a bridge between technology and music, there is a massive amount of technical data (MIDI structures, specific synth settings, and gear lists) that AI models have been trained on.

### Why Richie Hawtin is the Best AI Reference

* **Vast Dataset:** He has released exhaustive "Concept" series (like *Concept 1*) and albums like *Sheet One* and *Consumed* that are essentially masterclasses in minimal theory.
* **Specific Patch Data:** There is public documentation on his use of the **Roland TB-303**, **TR-808**, and **TR-909**. You can tell your AI to "emulate the Plastikman 303 filter resonance" or "recreate the Roland 101 melodic stabs from *Gak*."
* **Rhythmic Logic:** His style relies on **polyrhythms** and **offset percussion**. You can prompt your AI to "generate a 1/16th note hi-hat pattern with slight swing and velocity variations on the off-beats," which is a hallmark of his sound.

### Alternative: Robert Hood

If you want something more "raw" and "loop-based," Robert Hood is the second-best choice. His "Minimal Nation" style is famous for using **single-bar loops** with tiny variations in velocity and filter cutoff. AI producers excel at this because it requires mathematical precision rather than complex emotional transitions.

### 1. The Lead: "The Acid Squelch" (TB-303 Style)

The "Minimal" magic happens not in the notes, but in how the filter moves.

**MIDI Pattern (1 Bar Loop):**

* **Notes:** G1, G1, G2, G1, Bb1, G1, C2, G1 (All 16th notes)
* **Accents:** Place on the 1st, 5th, and 12th 16th notes.
* **Slides:** Slide from the 7th to the 8th note.

**Synthesis Parameters:**

* **Oscillator:** Single **Sawtooth** wave.
* **Filter Type:** 18dB or 24dB Low Pass Filter.
* **Cutoff:** Start at **15%**.
* **Resonance:** Set to **75-80%** (This is crucial for the "liquid" sound).
* **Envelope:** Decay at **40%**, Sustain at **0%**.
* **Automation:** Tell the AI to automate the **Cutoff Frequency** from **15% to 65%** over the course of 64 bars, then snap it back to 15%.

### 3. The Atmosphere: "The Feedback Loop"

Minimal tracks often use a single "ping" sent through a massive delay.

* **The Sound:** A short, high-pitched sine wave or "beep."

To keep a minimal track from becoming boring, you have to master the "slow burn." In the style of Richie Hawtin, the arrangement isn't about big "drops"—it's about the gradual evolution of a single sound.

Here is a **32-bar arrangement blueprint** designed to maximize hypnotic tension.

---

### 32-Bar Arrangement Structure

| Section | Bars | Primary Action | AI Production Focus |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **The Foundation** | 1–8 | **Kick & Rimshot Only** | Introduce the dry kick. Every 4th bar, add a tiny bit of **Reverb** to the Rimshot only, then snap it back to dry. |
| **The Introduction** | 9–16 | **Enter Closed Hats** | Bring in the 16th-note hats at **40% volume**. Slowly increase their volume (velocity) until bar 16. |
| **The Squelch** | 17–24 | **Enter TB-303 Pattern** | The synth enters with the **Filter Cutoff at 5%** (barely audible). It should sound like a muffled thumping. |
| **The Rise** | 25–28 | **Filter Automation** | Gradually open the Filter Cutoff from **5% to 40%**. The "acid" character should start to bite here. |
| **The Tension** | 29–31 | **The Wash** | Increase the **Delay Feedback** on the synth. The notes should start to overlap into a wall of sound. |
| **The Strip-Back** | 32 | **The "Ghost" Bar** | **Mute the Kick.** Leave only the "Ghost Percussion" and the Delay tail. Total silence on the 4th beat. |

---

### Pro-Tips for the AI Producer

1. **The "1% Rule":** Every 8 bars, change **one** tiny thing. It could be the decay of the kick (making it 10ms longer) or the panning of the hi-hats. In minimal, if the listener *notices* the change immediately, it's often too big.
2. **LFO Modulation:** Set a very slow LFO (spanning 16 bars) to slightly modulate the **Attack** of your percussion. This makes the drums feel "organic," as if they are breathing.
3. **Subtraction is Addition:** If the track feels stuck, don't add a new sound. Instead, take the existing synth pattern and **delete two notes**. The new "gap" in the rhythm often creates more energy than a new melody would.

---

### Final Polish: The "Ducking" Effect

Ensure your AI producer applies **Sidechain Compression** to the synth and the atmosphere, triggered by the Kick.

* **Ratio:** 4:1
* **Threshold:** -15dB to -20dB
* **Release:** Set to match the tempo (usually around **100ms** for 126 BPM) so the sound "pumps" back up just before the next kick hits.
