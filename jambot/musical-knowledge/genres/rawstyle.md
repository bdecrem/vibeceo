# Rawstyle
*The brutal evolution of hardstyle - pure power over euphoria*

**BPM Range:** 150-160  
**Parent Genre:** Hardstyle  
**Key Period:** 2010-present  
**Regional Origin:** Netherlands, Italy  

---

## Lineage

Rawstyle emerged in the early 2010s as a direct response to the increasingly euphoric and commercial direction of mainstream hardstyle. Pioneered by producers like **Radical Redemption**, **Warface**, **Delete**, and **E-Force**, rawstyle stripped away the uplifting melodies and hands-in-the-air euphoria that dominated hardstyle festivals, returning to the genre's underground roots.

The movement was partly a reaction against the "happy hardcore" direction that hardstyle had taken, with artists seeking to reclaim the aggressive, industrial edge that characterized early hardstyle pioneers like **The Prophet** and **Luna**. Rawstyle producers drew inspiration from hardcore techno, gabber, and industrial music, creating a sound that prioritized raw power and aggression over accessibility.

**Key evolutionary moments:**
- 2010-2012: Early rawstyle emergence with **Radical Redemption**'s "Brutal 6.0" series
- 2013-2015: Genre codification through labels like **Minus Is More** and **Theracords**  
- 2016-present: Mainstream adoption while maintaining underground edge

## Sonic DNA

### **The Rawstyle Kick**
The defining element of rawstyle is its kick drum - a heavily distorted, crushed, and saturated percussion hit that dominates the frequency spectrum. Unlike euphoric hardstyle's clean, punchy kicks, rawstyle kicks are:

- **Heavily saturated** with multiple layers of distortion
- **Frequency-rich** with aggressive mid-range content
- **Longer decay** creating a "crushing" effect
- **Anti-resonant** - designed to sound deliberately harsh and unpolished

### **Harmonic Characteristics**
- **Minimal melodic content** - often just single-note stabs or aggressive riffs
- **Dissonant intervals** when melody is present
- **Industrial tonalities** - metallic, grinding textures
- **Chromatic movement** rather than traditional harmonic progressions

### **Rhythmic Patterns**
- **Standard 4/4** with emphasis on beat 1 and 3
- **Syncopated offbeats** using harsh stabs and percussion
- **Build sections** focus on tension rather than euphoria
- **Anti-drop** philosophy - the drop is often more minimal than the build

## Sound Design

### **Kick Synthesis Techniques**
1. **Multi-layer approach:**
   - Low-end sine wave (50-80Hz)
   - Mid punch layer (200-800Hz) heavily saturated
   - High-end click/crack (2-8kHz) with extreme compression
   - Distorted tail extending 200-400ms

2. **Distortion chains:**
   - **Bitcrushing** for digital harshness
   - **Tape saturation** for warmth in low-end
   - **Waveshaping** for aggressive harmonic content
   - **Multiband distortion** allowing targeted frequency abuse

3. **Anti-mastering philosophy:**
   - Deliberately harsh frequency response
   - Compressed dynamics that fight traditional mixing wisdom
   - "Wall of sound" approach prioritizing impact over clarity

### **Screech and Stab Design**
- **Detuned oscillators** with heavy FM synthesis
- **Filtered noise** through resonant lowpass/bandpass filters
- **Granular synthesis** for stuttering, glitched textures
- **Reverse reverb** on lead stabs for aggressive attack

### **Industrial Texture Creation**
- **Field recordings** of machinery, construction, metal impacts
- **Granular processing** of industrial samples
- **Convolution reverb** using industrial spaces (factories, tunnels)
- **Rhythmic noise gates** creating mechanical patterns

## Production & Mixing

### **Frequency Management**
Unlike euphoric hardstyle's clean frequency separation, rawstyle embraces controlled chaos:

- **Mid-range saturation:** 200-2kHz deliberately overloaded
- **Bass competition:** Multiple elements fighting in low-end
- **Harsh high-end:** 4-8kHz pushed for aggression
- **Frequency masking** used as creative tool rather than avoided

### **Dynamics and Compression**
- **Aggressive limiting** with fast attack/release
- **Parallel compression** on drum groups
- **Sidechain pumping** less musical, more mechanical
- **Transient shaping** to emphasize aggressive attack phases

### **Spatial Design**
- **Mono low-end** for club system compatibility
- **Wide aggressive elements** in mid-range
- **Delayed reverb throws** for industrial space simulation
- **Binaural panning** effects during breakdown sections

### **Arrangement Philosophy**
1. **Anti-buildup:** Builds that increase tension without promising release
2. **False drops:** Moments that seem like drops but pull back
3. **Relentless pacing:** Less dynamic variation, more sustained intensity
4. **Minimal breakdown:** Short, industrial-textured break sections

## Reasoning Chains

### **Creative Decision Framework**

**When approaching melodic content:**
```
IF melody is present
THEN it must serve aggression, not beauty
→ Use dissonant intervals (tritones, minor 2nds)
→ Process through heavy distortion/bitcrushing
→ Keep duration short, impact high
```

**For kick design choices:**
```
GOAL: Maximum physical impact
→ Layer multiple distortion types
→ Extend decay for "crushing" effect
→ Sacrifice clarity for power
→ Test on club systems, not studio monitors
```

**Arrangement intensity management:**
```
START: High intensity baseline
NEVER drop below 70% intensity
CLIMAX: Achieved through texture addition, not dynamic contrast
RESOLUTION: Brief, quickly returning to tension
```

### **Genre Authenticity Checks**
1. **Does it sound "too clean"?** → Add more distortion
2. **Is it too melodic?** → Strip back harmonic content
3. **Would it work at a euphoric hardstyle festival?** → If yes, make it rawer
4. **Can you hear individual elements clearly?** → Add more frequency competition

### **Festival Presence**
While rawstyle emerged as an underground movement, it now commands significant festival presence:

- **Dedicated raw stages** at Defqon.1, Qlimax, Mysteryland
- **Raw-specific events:** "The Gathering," "Minus Is More" showcases
- **Club circuit:** Regular nights in Netherlands, Germany, Australia
- **International expansion:** Growing scenes in Australia, Chile, Russia

### **Production Community**
- **YouTube tutorials** by Delete, Radical Redemption
- **Sample pack culture:** Emphasis on kick construction
- **Hardware fetishism:** Analog distortion units, vintage samplers
- **Anti-VST sentiment:** Preference for outboard processing

### **Cultural Identity**
Rawstyle maintains its counter-culture identity despite mainstream success:
- **Anti-commercial messaging** in track titles and artwork
- **Military/industrial aesthetics** dominating visual identity
- **Underground club loyalty** even among chart-topping artists
- **Technical innovation** prioritized over commercial appeal
