# Wave (Trap Wave)
*Genre Study [11/16] - Electronic Music Production Research*

## Overview
**Parent Genre:** Trap/Future Bass  
**BPM Range:** 140-160  
**Peak Activity:** 2014-2018, with ongoing evolution  
**Regional Origins:** Internet-native, global scene  
**Key Characteristics:** Atmospheric pads, emotional chord progressions, cinematic builds, trap drums with future bass influences

---

## Lineage

### Historical Development
**2012-2013: Genesis**
- Emerged from the intersection of trap music's rhythmic foundation and future bass's melodic sensibilities
- Early pioneers began experimenting with more atmospheric, emotional approaches to trap production
- Influenced by film scoring techniques and ambient music traditions

**2014-2016: Golden Era**
- Genre crystallized around artists like Lorn, Holy Other, and early Shlohmo works
- SoundCloud became the primary distribution platform, enabling rapid scene development
- Characterized by longer track lengths and more developed arrangements than typical trap

**2017-2019: Evolution & Fragmentation**
- Subfragmentation into distinct styles: dark wave, melodic wave, ambient wave
- Influence spread to mainstream electronic music production
- Cross-pollination with genres like melodic dubstep and progressive house

**2020-Present: Modern Context**
- Integration into broader "emotional trap" and "cinematic bass" movements
- Revival through TikTok and streaming platform algorithmic discovery
- Influence on contemporary hip-hop and R&B production

## Sonic DNA

### Core Elements
**Atmospheric Foundation**
- Lush reverb tails creating sense of infinite space
- Ambient pad layers with slow attack and long decay
- Field recordings and found sounds for textural depth
- White noise sweeps and vinyl crackle for analog warmth

**Harmonic Language**
- Minor key preferences (Am, Dm, Em most common)
- Extended chords (add9, sus2, sus4) for emotional ambiguity
- Modal interchange from natural minor to dorian/aeolian
- Chromatic bass movement for dramatic tension

**Rhythmic Architecture**
- Half-time trap rhythms (140-160 BPM feeling like 70-80)
- Syncopated hi-hats with ghost snares
- 808 kicks with long sub-bass tails
- Polyrhythmic percussion layers creating groove complexity

**Dynamic Contour**
- Extended build-ups (32-64 bars typical)
- Dramatic drops with full frequency spectrum revelation
- Breakdown sections featuring isolated melodic elements
- Arrangement focused on emotional journey over dancefloor functionality

## Sound Design

### Signature Textures
**Atmospheric Pads**
- **Serum/Massive patches:** Saw/square waves with heavy unison detune
- **Processing chain:** High-pass at 200Hz → Chorus → Long reverb → Tape saturation
- **Modulation:** Slow LFO on filter cutoff (1/8 note rate)
- **Layering technique:** 3-4 pad layers in different octaves with varying brightness

**Emotional Lead Synthesis**
- **Waveform selection:** Saw waves with subtle FM modulation
- **Filter envelope:** Slow attack, medium decay, high sustain, medium release
- **Effects processing:** Stereo delay (1/8 dotted) → Reverb (2.5s decay) → Subtle distortion
- **Performance techniques:** Heavy use of pitch bend and vibrato automation

**Percussion Programming**
- **808 Processing:** Multiband compression, saturation on upper harmonics
- **Hi-hat patterns:** 32nd note triplet rolls with velocity automation
- **Snare layering:** Acoustic snare + digital clap + white noise burst
- **Rimshot placement:** On off-beats for rhythmic complexity

**Cinematic Elements**
- **String arrangements:** Real orchestral samples layered with synthesized strings
- **Vocal processing:** Heavily reverbed vocal chops with formant shifting
- **Impact sounds:** Reversed cymbals, sub drops, orchestral hits
- **Textural layers:** Film dialogue, rain sounds, analog hiss

## Production & Mixing

### Arrangement Philosophy
**32-Bar Structure Approach**
- **Intro (32 bars):** Atmospheric build with gradual element introduction
- **Verse/Build (32 bars):** Rhythmic foundation with melodic development
- **Chorus/Drop (32 bars):** Full arrangement with emotional climax
- **Breakdown (16-32 bars):** Stripped arrangement highlighting key melodies
- **Final Build/Outro (32-64 bars):** Intensified return with extended resolution

**Frequency Spectrum Management**
- **Sub-bass (20-60Hz):** 808 kick and bass lines, heavily compressed
- **Low-mids (60-250Hz):** Careful carving to avoid mud, side-chain compression
- **Mids (250Hz-2kHz):** Pad layers and harmonic content, subtle saturation
- **High-mids (2-8kHz):** Lead elements and vocal presence
- **Highs (8-20kHz):** Atmospheric textures and stereo width enhancement

**Spatial Processing**
- **Reverb architecture:** Multiple reverb sends (hall, plate, spring)
- **Stereo imaging:** Wide pads, centered bass, rhythmic elements in stereo field
- **Depth creation:** Near-field leads, mid-field pads, far-field atmospherics
- **Movement programming:** Autopan on atmospheric elements, static groove elements

### Mixing Techniques
**Dynamic Processing**
- **Multiband compression:** Gentle 2:1 ratios across frequency bands
- **Side-chain compression:** 1/4 note pumping from 808 kick
- **Parallel compression:** Aggressive parallel chain on drum bus
- **Limiting approach:** Conservative limiting preserving dynamic range

**Creative Effects Usage**
- **Granular delays:** Texture creation on atmospheric elements
- **Chorus/dimension:** Width enhancement on pad layers
- **Tape saturation:** Warmth on mix bus and key melodic elements
- **Bit crushing:** Subtle degradation for lo-fi character

**Automation Philosophy**
- **Filter sweeps:** Long-form automation on pad filters
- **Reverb send automation:** Dynamic space creation
- **Stereo width modulation:** Movement in atmospheric layers
- **Volume automation:** Hand-drawn curves for organic feel

## Reasoning Chains

### Emotional Architecture
**Tension-Release Patterns**
```
Setup → Build Tension → Peak Emotional Moment → Gentle Resolution
(16-32 bars) → (32-64 bars) → (16-32 bars) → (32+ bars)
```

**Harmonic Progression Logic**
- Start with stable tonic
- Introduce mild dissonance through extended chords  
- Build to dominant tension or modal interchange
- Resolve to tonic or unexpected modal resolution
- *Example: Am → F(add9) → C/G → G/B → Am*

**Arrangement Development**
- **Layer Introduction Timing:** New elements every 8-16 bars
- **Textural Density Curves:** Gradual increase to climax, sudden reduction for contrast
- **Rhythmic Complexity Evolution:** Simple foundation building to polyrhythmic climax
- **Melodic Development:** Theme introduction → variation → counterpoint → resolution

### Production Decision Trees
**When to add atmospheric elements:**
- If mix feels too focused on rhythm → add ambient textures
- If arrangement feels too busy → create space with reverb sends
- If emotional impact insufficient → layer additional harmonic content
- If stereo field too narrow → add wide atmospheric pads

**EQ Decision Making:**
- Low-mid buildup → high-pass non-bass elements at 80-200Hz
- Harsh frequencies → gentle cuts at 2-4kHz range
- Lack of air → boost at 8-12kHz with shelf EQ
- Muddy low-end → side-chain everything to 808 kick

**Arrangement Problem-Solving:**
- Energy drop in breakdown → maintain one rhythmic element
- Repetitive 8-bar loops → introduce variation every 16 bars
- Weak transitions → use reverse reverb swells and drum fills
- Predictable structure → vary section lengths (24 bars vs 32 bars)

**Lorn - "Acid Rain" (2012)**

- *Production notes:* Masterful use of space, minimal but effective sound design

## Current Scene

### Platform Distribution
**Primary Platforms:**
- **SoundCloud:** Original home, still active for underground releases
- **YouTube:** Visual-focused releases, often with animated/cinematic videos  
- **Spotify/Apple Music:** Mainstream wave artists and playlists
- **Bandcamp:** Niche releases and full album experiences

**Playlist Culture:**
- "Chill Trap" playlists often feature wave tracks
- "Emotional Bass" and "Cinematic Electronic" categories
- Study/work playlists incorporating ambient wave subgenres
- Gaming and streaming background music integration

### Production Community
**Educational Resources:**
- YouTube tutorials focusing on Serum/Massive pad creation
- Splice sample packs featuring wave-style elements
- Producer communities on Discord and Reddit (r/trapproduction)
- Beat tape culture maintaining DIY aesthetic

**Hardware/Software Preferences:**
- **DAWs:** FL Studio, Ableton Live, Logic Pro X
- **Synths:** Serum, Massive, Omnisphere for atmospheric elements
- **Effects:** Valhalla VintageVerb, FabFilter Pro-Q 3, Soundtoys EchoBoy
- **Sample sources:** Splice, Loopmasters, custom field recordings

**Collaboration Patterns:**
- Producer-rapper partnerships (similar to traditional trap)
- Remix culture with heavy atmospheric reinterpretations
- Beat tape compilation releases
- Cross-genre collaborations with ambient and post-rock artists

### Regional Variations
**West Coast US:** More ambient-influenced, post-rock crossover elements
**Southeast US:** Heavier trap influence, darker atmospheric choices
**European Scene:** Techno-influenced rhythmic complexity, longer arrangements
**International:** Fusion with local electronic music traditions (UK garage rhythms, Asian ambient traditions)

### Contemporary Challenges
**Streaming Algorithm Optimization:**
- Tracks often edited to 2-3 minutes for playlist inclusion
- Hook placement optimized for first 30 seconds
- Balance between artistic vision and algorithmic performance

**Genre Boundary Blurring:**
- Overlap with melodic dubstep causing identity confusion
- Integration into mainstream trap reducing distinctiveness
- Rise of "emotional trap" as umbrella term encompassing wave

**Production Standardization:**
- Template culture reducing creative experimentation
- Sample pack overuse creating homogenous sound
- Need for innovation within established framework
